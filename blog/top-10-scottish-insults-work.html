Top 10 Scottish Insults You Can Actually Say at Work

May 2025

Let's face it: sometimes work can be frustrating. But before you reach for the standard office expletives, why not try something with a bit more Scottish flair? Here are 10 Scottish insults that are witty enough to use in the workplace without getting you into HR trouble.

1. "You're a right numpty"

Perfect for when someone does something particularly silly. It's playful enough to be taken as a joke but still gets the point across that they've done something foolish.

2. "Away and bile yer heid"

Literally meaning "go and boil your head," this is a classic Scottish way of telling someone to go away and think about what they've done. Great for those moments when you need a colleague to reconsider their approach.

3. "Ye're a wee glaikit"

When someone's being a bit slow on the uptake, this is the perfect way to point it out. It means they're being a bit daft or foolish, but in a more charming way than saying it directly.

4. "That's pure dead brilliant"

Okay, this one's not really an insult, but it's a great way to sarcastically compliment someone's work when it's actually not up to scratch. The Scottish way of saying "well, that's just great..."

5. "Ye're a right bampot"

This one's for those colleagues who are just a bit... eccentric. It's a gentle way of saying someone's a bit of a character without being too harsh.

6. "Away and don't talk mince"

Perfect for those meetings where someone's talking nonsense. It's a polite way of saying "stop talking rubbish" without being too direct.

7. "Ye're a right jobby"

This one's a bit cheeky but still workplace-appropriate. It's a playful way of calling someone a bit of a fool.

8. "That's a load of keech"

When someone's idea is just plain wrong, this is a more colorful way of saying it's nonsense. It's a bit more fun than just saying "that's wrong."

9. "Ye're a right walloper"

This one's for those colleagues who are being particularly annoying. It's a bit more playful than calling them a pain in the neck.

10. "Away and chase yersel"

This is a great way to tell someone to get lost without being too rude. Perfect for those moments when you need someone to leave you alone to get your work done.

Remember: While these insults are relatively mild, always consider your workplace culture and the relationship you have with your colleagues before using them. The best Scottish insults are delivered with a smile and a twinkle in the eye!
